Note-taking is an activity that occurs in the present moment, but is in service of a future one.

Taking notes is about collecting information and ideas in *this* moment, and making them available for our *future* selves to act upon. We’re seeding ideas now, and helping them grow stronger as we explore and learn.

After all,

Ideas improve by writing about them, not by thinking about them.

Notes should be atomic

Create notes about one thing, but capture the entirety of that thing. The idea is to self-contain the note so it’s clear and concise, and able to be fully understood in any future context.



Notes should be concept-oriented

Orienting by concept makes it easier to gather context in the future and build associations between multiple notes. Especially between the notes that appear to be unrelated, but in a new, future context, become linked.



Notes should be densely linked

Connect notes bidirectionally to help spark new ideas based on related contexts



Notes should be emergent

Let structure emerge over time. Just take notes and start to connect them. This will lead to new ideas, new organization, and new output.



Notes should be titled like APIs

The title is an abstraction for the note itself. Titles provide a window into the content by being able to contain the idea itself while providing the ability to dive deeper into the idea when you want.

The goal is to build a body of work that, over time, compounds on itself, offering future returns greater than the sum of their parts. Like any long-term investment strategy, the more you put in on a consistent basis, the better your returns will likely be over time.

Note taking allows us to seed initial ideas, tend to them over time, and eventually, harvest the best ideas the moment they turn ripe. It’s this analogy that leads people to build digital gardens for tending and nurturing ideas—a fascinating practice I’m actively exploring for myself.

There are good and not-so-good ways to take notes, and I’m in the early stages of researching and designing what works for me. I’ll be capturing what I learn and sharing on a regular basis.
